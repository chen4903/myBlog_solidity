Euler Finance & Flashloan

KEY WORDS: Flashloan

ATTACK TIME: Mar-13-2023 08:56:35 AM +UTC

LOSSES：$187 million

Event Background

  • Euler Finance is a flashloan protocol
  • attack digest:
    • The hack was made possible by a liquidity issue in the DonateToReserve function of the eToken.
    • Euler’s hacker took advantage of these inconsistencies to create a false impression that the platform had a low amount of deposited eTokens and fake debt due to the fact that the dTokens were not burned.

How Euler Finance runs?

digest:

  • eToken is an asset token that represents a user’s collateral. For example, if a user uses 10 ETHs as collateral, they can obtain 10 eETHs

  • dToken is an asset token that represents the principal and interest of a user’s loan. In the loan agreement of Euler Finance, the funds borrowed by the user are converted into dTokens and distributed to the user’s wallet address. The value of dToken is equal to the sum of the loan principal and interest

  • Users can deposit their cryptocurrency assets into the Euler Finance agreement as collateral to obtain loan limits.

  • Users can use the borrowed funds for other investment or trading activities, or withdraw them to their own wallet address for use.
    The borrowed funds will generate a certain amount of interest, which is determined by the market supply and demand relationship and calculated through smart contracts and automatically settled.
  • If the value of the borrowed assets drops, users need to supplement the collateral within a certain period of time to maintain the borrowing ratio. If it cannot be supplemented, it may be forced to liquidate, and the collateral will be sold to repay the loan principal and interest.

For example:

  • Assuming Alice has 10 Ethereum (ETH) assets and wishes to use them for trading or other investment activities, but currently does not have enough cash on hand. She can use Euler Finance’s lending function to use these 10 ETHs as collateral to obtain a certain loan limit, such as $1000.
  • On the Euler Finance platform, Alice can choose the loan amount and term, while also paying a certain amount of interest and collateral fees. Assuming she chooses a loan of $1000 with a term of 30 days and an interest rate of 5%.
  • At this point, Alice will receive a loan of $1000 and bear an annual interest of 5%, which is $50. After 30 days, she needs to repay the loan principal of $1000 and interest of $50, totaling $1050. If she can repay within the deadline, she can retrieve the collateral, which is 10 ETHs.
  • If the market value of ETH drops when the loan term expires, making the collateral value lower than the sum of the loan principal and interest, Alice needs to supplement sufficient collateral within the term to maintain the loan ratio. If she is unable to supplement, she may be forced to liquidate and the collateral will be sold to repay the loan principal and interest.
  • Through Euler Finance’s lending function, Alice can obtain sufficient funds for other investment or trading activities without selling ETH, while also controlling her own risks and flexibly selecting lending amounts and terms based on market supply and demand.

Tx Details

This incident involved multiple attacks on transactions, all of which utilized the same contract vulnerability for attacks. For this, we choose one of the pool transactions.

Network: Mainnet

One of tx hash: 0xc310a0affe2169d1f6feec1c63dbc7f7c62a887fa48795d327d4d2da2d6b111d

EOA attacker: 0xb66cd966670d962c227b3eaba30a872dbfb995db

contract attacker: 0x036cec1a199234fc02f72d29e596a09440825f1c

Attack Process

digest of attack:

  • The attacker takes a flash loan and deposits it into Euler.
  • Euler mints tokens for the deposit.
  • The attacker donates these minted tokens, making them eligible for liquidation.
  • The attacker liquidates themselves for profit.

details:

(1) Hacker flashloaned 30M DAI from Aave, and then deployed two contracts: loan contract(Contract 6c72) and settlled contract(Contract a0b3)

(2) deposit and loan 20M DAI in Euler Protocol contract to derive 19.5M eDAI

(3) call mint() to loan 200M dDAI and 195.6 eDAI

(4) repaied 10M DAI and burned 10M dDAI, then call mint() again to loan 200M dDAI and 195.6 eDAI。

(5) call donateToReserves() with 100M eDAI, settle with liquidate() for getting 310M dDAI and 250M eDAI

(6) withdraw 38.9M DAI and repaied 30M DAI from Aave. As a result,hacker derived 8.87M DAI

Vulnerability Analysis

the core problems: checkLiquidity is the most important part of repayment of funds, liquidity checking and debt liquidation. We can found that it appearred in mint() and transferFrom().

(1) Hacker got 38.8M DAI by donateToReserves(), let’s scrutinize it: donating eDAI and return DAI, but it lacks of liquidity check.

(2) comparing with mint(), we found that donateToReserves() lacks of checkLiquidity()

(3) checkLiquidity() contains callInternalModul() which will inspect eDAI > dDAI. Hack found this problem and avoided liquidity check.

conclusion:

  • logical vulnerability
  • donateToReserves() lacks checkLiquidity() checking liquidity status of the borrower
  • Reduce eTokens representing collateral, resulting in liquidation. Due to the small number of eTokens representing collateral, very few original assets were deducted, but the original collateral assets only decreased very little .All of it is due to the lack of inspection of liquidity and asset liquidity.
